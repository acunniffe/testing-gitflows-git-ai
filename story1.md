# The Cartographer of Forgotten Roads

Elena had a gift that no one believed until they saw it for themselves: she could draw maps of places she had never been.

It started when she was seven, sketching a path through the woods behind her grandmother's house — a path that didn't exist yet. Her grandmother found it three days later, revealed by a fallen oak that had cleared the brush in exactly the shape Elena had drawn.

By thirty, she had filled eleven notebooks with roads, trails, and passages that shouldn't have been possible. A staircase carved into the side of a glacier in Patagonia. A tunnel beneath the streets of Lisbon that connected two fountains built centuries apart. A bridge in rural Japan that appeared only during the autumn fog.

People hired her, eventually. Not governments or corporations — they didn't believe in her — but individuals. A woman searching for her estranged brother. A retired sailor looking for an island he'd visited once in a dream. A child who insisted there was a door in her closet that led somewhere important.

Elena would sit with them, listen to their stories, and then close her eyes. Her hand would move across the paper without hesitation, as if taking dictation from the geography itself.

She never followed her own maps. That was her one rule.

Until the day she drew a map she hadn't been asked for. She woke at 3 a.m. and found it already finished on her desk — a route starting from her own front door, winding through the city, and ending at a small park she had never visited. At the center of the park, she had drawn a single X and written two words beneath it: *come home*.

She recognized the handwriting. It was her mother's, who had been dead for twelve years.

Elena put on her coat, folded the map into her pocket, and stepped outside. The night air was cool and still. She followed the first turn, then the second.

She never came back. But sometimes, in cities she had mapped for others, a new road appears overnight — one that wasn't there before, leading somewhere no one can quite explain. The locals learn to trust it. The roads she leaves behind have never led anyone astray.
